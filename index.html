Synthetic Ornithology — Listening Index

This site gathers audio examples generated by EAGLE (Environmental Audio Generation for Localised Ecologies), presented as part of the research project Synthetic Ornithology. The clips are grouped to support reflective listening: how simulation can sound plausible, where it becomes strange, and what those edges can teach us about ecological imagination.

  • Comparisons — Paired listening between recorded soundscapes and generated outputs
  • Notable Cases — Moments of compelling ambiguity or emergent behaviour
  • Failure Modes — Audible artefacts and misclassifications as creative evidence

Listening notes

  • Context matters. These examples are conditioned by place, time and weather. Read the clip labels.
  • Plausibility vs. truth. Some outputs sound “right” while being technically impossible; this tension is intentional.

About

Synthetic Ornithology treats machine learning not as a mere tool but as a compositional process. The examples here illustrate three facets of that process: correspondence (Comparisons), emergence (Notable Cases), and breakdown (Failure Modes). Together they map a listening field between realism and speculation.
